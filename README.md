# Retail ETF Participation: The New Direction of FI-RV Premia?

## Overview

This presentation explores a simple question:

As traditional fixed-income relative-value (FI-RV) opportunities become increasingly competitive, where might the next generation of inefficiencies emerge?

The project examines how the evolution of fixed-income markets has progressed from:

1. Cash-Futures Basis and Financing Trades
2. Volatility Premia and Derivative Distortions
3. Potential Retail-Driven Market Structure Effects

The central hypothesis is that increasing retail participation in fixed-income ETFs may alter the structure of future relative-value opportunities.

---

## Research Question

Historically, many FI-RV strategies focused on:

- Repo specials
- Financing carry
- Cash-futures basis
- Treasury relative value
- Swap spread relationships

As these opportunities become increasingly efficient, institutional participants have expanded into volatility and derivative-based relative value.

This project asks:

> What happens when retail investors begin participating at scale in bond ETF ecosystems?

Examples include:

- TLT
- IEF
- HYG
- LQD

and their associated options markets.

---

## Core Thesis

Market inefficiencies are often created not only by instruments, but by participants.

As retail participation enters a niche market, several structural changes often occur:

- Increased volume
- Deeper liquidity
- Expanded option chains
- Greater willingness to chase price action
- More flow-driven pricing behavior

The presentation investigates whether these changes could create a new source of fixed-income relative-value premia.

---

## Proposed Framework

A conceptual strategy discussed in the presentation is:

### Sell Rich Markets

Provide liquidity in heavily trafficked retail products:

- Bond ETF options
- Vertical spreads
- TLT options
- HYG options
- LQD options
- IEF options

### Buy Cheap Markets

Use collected premium to gain exposure through less crowded institutional markets:

- CME Treasury options
- Interest-rate swaps
- Total return swaps
- Credit default swaps
- Deep OTM Treasury futures options

The idea can be summarized as:

> Sell the rich markets.
>
> Buy the cheap markets.
